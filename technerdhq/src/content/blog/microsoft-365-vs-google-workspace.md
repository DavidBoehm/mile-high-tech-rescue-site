## Quick Overview: What You're Actually Getting

Both suites give you the essentials: professional email, cloud storage, video conferencing, calendars, and collaboration tools. But they approach things very differently.

| Feature | Microsoft 365 | Google Workspace |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Email** | Outlook (robust, desktop app) | Gmail (familiar, web-first) |
| **Documents** | Word, Excel, PowerPoint | Docs, Sheets, Slides |
| **Storage** | 1TB (Business Basic+) | 30GB–Unlimited depending on plan |
| **Video Calls** | Teams | Meet |
| **Desktop Apps** | ✅ Full Office suite included | ❌ Web-based only |
| **Collaboration** | Real-time co-editing | Built for collaboration from day one |
| **Best For** | Traditional offices, heavy Excel/PowerPoint users | Remote teams, startups, Gmail lovers |

---

## Microsoft 365: The Powerhouse for Traditional Offices

### The Good Stuff

If your team lives in Excel spreadsheets, builds complex PowerPoint decks, or needs serious desktop software muscle, **Microsoft 365** is tough to beat.

- **Familiar desktop apps** — Word, Excel, and PowerPoint that actually run *on* your computer (not just in a browser)
- **Outlook** — Still the gold standard for **best business email 2025**, especially if you manage heavy email volume
- **OneDrive & SharePoint** — Robust file storage with serious enterprise features
- **Microsoft Teams** — A collaboration hub that does *everything* (sometimes too much)
- **Advanced security** — Built-in compliance tools that regulated industries need

### The Trade-offs

- **Price** — Generally more expensive, especially if you want the desktop apps
- **Learning curve** — Teams can feel overwhelming
- **Web experience** — The online versions aren't as smooth as Google's

---

## Google Workspace: Built for the Modern, Agile Team

### The Good Stuff

Google Workspace is all about simplicity and speed. If your team values getting things done quickly over feature depth, this is your jam.

- **Gmail for business** — Clean, fast, and your team probably already knows it
- **Real-time collaboration** — Google Docs practically invented live co-editing
- **Works everywhere** — No software to install; just open a browser tab
- **Simple pricing** — Straightforward plans without too many confusing tiers
- **Great mobile apps** — Android and iOS apps are genuinely excellent

### The Trade-offs

- **No desktop Office apps** — If you need heavy-duty Excel, you'll be frustrated
- **Fewer enterprise features** — Advanced compliance and security require higher-tier plans
- **File organization** — Google Drive isn't as structured as SharePoint

## So… Which One Should You Pick?

### Choose **Microsoft 365** if:
- Your team lives in Excel, Word, and PowerPoint
- You need advanced security and compliance features
- You prefer desktop software over browser tools
- Your industry has strict data regulations

### Choose **Google Workspace** if:
- You want something simple your team can learn in 5 minutes
- Real-time collaboration is your #1 priority
- You're a startup or fully remote team
- You love Gmail and don't want to change
